**Virginia Trueheart**
 
 **Director of User Services, Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC)**
 
 Virginia Trueheart is a seasoned professional in the High-Performance Computing (HPC) field, currently serving as the Director of User Services at the Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC). With a strong background in IT services and consulting, Virginia has dedicated her career to facilitating the integration of HPC technologies into various research domains, particularly in climate data and analytics.
 
 Virginia's experience in the HPC industry is underscored by her participation in the HPCintheCity2021 hackathons. This hands-on experience has not only deepened her understanding of the technical challenges faced by researchers but also instilled in her a unique perspective on mentoring and HPC education. Her involvement in these hackathons has equipped her with the ability to bridge the gap between technical expertise and practical application, making her an invaluable resource for faculty and research staff.
 
 Virginia's current research focus is centered on leveraging HPC to enhance climate modeling and prediction. Her work involves the analysis of massive datasets from various sources, including satellite observations and weather stations, to create more accurate and detailed weather models. This research is crucial for understanding and mitigating the impacts of climate change, and it aligns perfectly with the broader goals of the HPC community.
 
 As a mentor, Virginia brings a unique blend of technical acumen and educational expertise. Her experience as an undergraduate researcher in a cutting-edge field has given her a profound appreciation for the challenges and opportunities that come with advancing HPC technologies. She is well-positioned to guide students and researchers through the complexities of HPC, ensuring they can effectively harness its power to drive innovation in climate science and beyond.
 
 Virginia's contributions to the HPC community are multifaceted. Her ability to communicate complex technical concepts in an accessible manner makes her an excellent mentor for those new to the field. Her participation in hackathons has also instilled in her a passion for collaborative problem-solving, which she brings to every mentoring session. By fostering a culture of innovation and collaboration, Virginia aims to empower the next generation of HPC professionals to tackle the most pressing challenges in climate science and beyond.
 
 In summary, Virginia Trueheart is a dedicated and accomplished professional in the HPC field, with a deep understanding of both the technical and educational aspects of high-performance computing. Her unique blend of experience, skills, and passion for mentoring makes her an exceptional addition to any HPC community, poised to make significant contributions to the advancement of climate data and analytics through her work at TACC.
